Körner József

Körner József ( Budapest , 1907 . March 17 - Budapest, 1971 . February 3rd ) Hungarian architect of CIAM member of the Hungarian team, later in the socialist realism representative, the Kossuth Prize (1959).


Career


In the first phase of his career, he joined the efforts of modern architecture; he became a member of the Hungarian group of CIAM . As an artist not supported by the cultural policy of the age, he was only commissioned to design residential houses. After the Second World War, he received several state assignments: he designed the Dagály Street Bath (Budapest, 1948, with Lajos Darvas) and the headquarters of the Ministry of Foreign Trade on Honvéd Street (Budapest, 1948). In 1952, he designed the II. district party house, which later became the building of the council (now the district municipality). The building is a typical example of socialist realist architecture. In 1956, he took part in the planning of the first phase of the Lágymányos housing estate . His late works again show modern features, such as the Tatabányalibrary (1965) and a hotel building in Veszprém (1967)
