Lajos Zalaváry

Váry Louis County ( Budapest , 1923 . June 1 - Budapest , 2018 . February 13 ), the National Arts awarded the title, Ybl - and Kossuth Prize-winning Hungarian architect.


Career


Lajos Zalaváry was born on June 1, 1923 in Budapest. In 1942 he enrolled in the Faculty of Architecture of the József Nádor Technical University . His studies were interrupted by the war. He and his companions, including Zoltán Farkasdy and György Jánossy , were taken to Germany in 1944 . Returning home - still as a student - he worked in the offices of architects such as Jenő Kismarty-Lechner , Pál Virágh , György Rácz . Continuing his university studies, he graduated in architecture in 1949 . His teachers were, among others, Iván Kotsis and Károly Weichinger . In his dissertation, he had to design a building for a Sculpture Museum.


After graduating, in 1949, he started working in István Janáky's studio in the Public Building Design Office (KÖZTI), which separated from MATI . In addition to Janáky - with Zoltán Farkasdy and György Jánossy - he participated in the planning of the University of Heavy Industry in Miskolc . He was there when the master school of MÉSZ was established in 1953 under the leadership of Janáky , which was also a student of the first cycle between 1953-55, and later became the leading architect of the IV-VI cycle.


During his entire professional career, Zalaváry worked at KÖZTI , initially id. In Janáky's studio, then from 1965 as a studio manager. Until his retirement, he was a senior architect at the Public Building Design Company.


He is a founding member of the Association of Hungarian Architects. Since 1950 he has been a lecturer at the Department of Public Building Design of BUTE, the leading architect of the MÉSZ Master School (cycles IV-VI), a member of the Hungarian Academy of Arts ( 2001 ).
